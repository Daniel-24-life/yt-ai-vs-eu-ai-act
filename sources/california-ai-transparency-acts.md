# California AI Transparency Acts: Source Summary

> Extracted: 2026-06-09
> Purpose: Dual-anchor blog (YT 6/2 × EU 8/2) California fact base

## Two Key Bills

### AB-2655 (Elections: Deepfakes)

- **Signed**: 2024-09-17 by Gov. Newsom
- **What it did**: Required large online platforms (≥1M California users) to label and remove election-related deceptive deepfakes
- **Status**: **Permanently enjoined** 2025-08-20 by Judge Mendez (Kohls v. Bonta) — Section 230 preemption
- **Significance**: "Mandatory platform removal" path struck down in US courts

### SB-942 / AB-853 (California AI Transparency Act)

- **SB-942 signed**: 2024-09-17
- **SB-942 effective**: 2026-01-01
- **AB-853 effective**: 2026-08-02 (same date as EU Art.50)
- **Covers**: Generative AI providers with ≥1M monthly active users (image/video/audio; text exempted)
- **Requirements**:
  1. Free AI Detection Tool (with API access)
  2. Mandatory latent disclosure (embedded in content)
  3. Optional manifest disclosure (visible to users)
- **Penalty**: $5,000/day per violation, AG enforcement + civil remedy

## Key Insight: One Failed, One Succeeded

- AB-2655 tried "forced removal" → struck down (Section 230 preemption)
- SB-942 uses "label + disclose + tools" → enacted and now in force
- **Conclusion**: The viable enforcement path for GenAI governance is "label + disclose + tools", not "forced removal"

## Alignment with YouTube 6/2

YouTube's detect + disclose + subject complaint mechanism simultaneously aligns with:
- EU Article 50 (transparency obligations)
- China 标识办法 (content distribution platform verification obligations)
- California SB-942 (disclosure + detection tool path)
