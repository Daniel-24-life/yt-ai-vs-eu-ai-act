## Three Detection Triggers

1. **YouTube's own GenAI tools** (Veo, Fantasia Canvas, etc.) — auto-tagged
2. **C2PA metadata detected** in externally uploaded content — auto-tagged
3. **Internal classifier** flags suspected AI-synthesized likeness — auto-tagged

Creators have **no opt-out** from auto-detection labels.

## Disclosure Toggle vs Auto-Detection

- **Toggle** (2024-03 → 2025-05-21 mandatory): Creator manually declares AI use
- **Auto-detection** (2026-06-02): Platform independently identifies and labels AI content regardless of toggle

The 6/2 shift moved from "creator self-declares" to "platform independently detects" — the compliance burden shifted from voluntary to enforced.

## Label Visibility

From May 2026, YouTube moved AI labels to "spots viewers will actually see" — player overlay + description area (PPC Land 2026-05-30).

## Penalty Mechanism

- YPP revenue impact
- Content removal for non-compliance
- No government-level fines (platform-enforced only)
